About Me

I am an Astrophysicist at the Pennsylvania State University. My work involves characterization of stars that may host planets that have the right environment to support life. I am part of the team looking for Earth 2.0.

My other work involves understanding the Physics of stars in a binary system. This work is fundamental to our understanding of how stars are born, evolve, and eventually die. Knowledge gained from this work will help astronomers determine the mass of galaxies and shed light on dark matter.

I am also part of the team building the $3.3 Million infrared instrument, Habitable Zone Planet Finder (HPF) at Penn State. This is the first infrared instrument ever built in the US and second in the world. With the 10m Hobby Eberly Telescope, HPF will discover Earth-like planets around red stars.
